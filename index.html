About the Contest

Android Masters is a collegiate inter-school Android app development challenge. It is open to all universities and colleges across the Philippines. The main objective of the program is to encourage students to develop their creative app ideas and publish it to Google Play Store.

Mechanics
  • To join Android Masters, create a team composed of at most 3 students and a faculty mentor all coming from the same school/college/university. Students must be officially enrolled at the time of registration.
  • There are no limits to the number of app entries per school. Teams can submit more than 1 entries across different categories.
  • It is a strict requirement that all entries should be published in the Google Play Store.
  • There are no limits to the number of teams handled by the mentors.
  • Top 5 entries per category will be invited for the culminating event in Manila. Teams will pitch and demo their application to a panel of judges.

Frequently Asked Questions
Can we submit finished apps but not yet published on Google Play Store?
Yes, as long these apps were not submitted as part of any contest.
Where can I learn how to develop Android mobile applications?
You can visit the following websites to jumpstart learning in Android Development: Android Developer Official Site and Udacity courses Android for Beginners and Android Fundamentals.
How can we publish our apps?
To publish your apps on Google Play, you need to register for a Google Play developer account. Once registered, you can access the Google Play Developer Console. This is where you will upload and manage your Android apps.
Is there a fee for creating a Google Play developer account?
There is a one-time fee of $25 (less than 1, 250 pesos) for creating an account using a credit card or a debit card. You can you can use GCash MasterCard, Paymaya Card, or other prepaid virtual cards if you do not have a credit nor debit card.
Can we still update the apps we published even after we submitted them?
Yes, you may do so before the deadline of submissions. The evaluation of the apps will start after the deadline.
I have additional questions about Android Masters. How may I contact you?
Please send us an email at gdgphilippines@gmail.com with the subject "Android Masters Inquiry" or post your question in the Android Masters 2016 Google Space.
Android Masters 2015

A total of 72 teams from all over the country joined the first inter-school Android app development challenge. These teams all had a goal: to be crowned the first ever Android Masters.

Out of 72, only 10 entries have made to the grand finals that was held in PLDT Innolab, Mandaluyong City last October 23. Each of the finalists were given seven minutes to present their apps in front of distinguished judges from the tech industry and eager audiences who was excited to see some of the greatest ideas come into reality.

Meanwhile, the audience who went to the venue to support their favorite apps also enjoyed with talks from Jomar Tigcal and Andreas Galster on App Indexing and Material design aside from the good food, goodies from our sponsors and the opportunity to experience and play around with the apps of the finalists.

This year, expect more ideas from students all over the country come to life as we once again fire up the competition.
